pomodoro

get started

the technique

The pomodoro technique is a useful strategy for working created by Francesco Cirillo about thirty years ago. The strategy consists of intervals of working and breaks. Inspired by the tomato-shaped timer Cirillo had, each interval is called a "pomodoro", Italian for the red vegetable.

how it works

The technique allows a period of work that usually ranges from a twenty to twenty-five minutes, followed by a short break. The break typically lasts about three to five minutes. After four periods of work, or "pomodoros", there's a longer break that lasts about fifteen to twenty minutes.

timer instructions

It should be rather self explanatory, but the timer values along with the pomodoro value can be changed when the timer is paused. The "start/pause" and "work" buttons can be pressed. The "start/pause" button controls the timer and the "work" button toggles between whether the user should be working or resting. The tomato itself will grow, ripen or fall depending on the state of the timer.
